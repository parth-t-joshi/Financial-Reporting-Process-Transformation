# DMAIC Root Cause Identification: Fishbone Diagram Summary

**The Defect:** Weekly SG&A Expenses reporting cycle requires **48 hours to complete** and Monthly **36 hours to complete**, hindering real-time decision-making.

| Category (Rib)  | Primary Root Causes & Process Drivers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            | Link to DMAIC Waste (Muda)                                    | Impact on Final Dashboard (.pbix)                                                                                   |
| :-------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Process**     | • Fragmented data ingestion across separate 2025/2026 Monthly & Weekly folders.<br><br>• Manual normalization of split-week transaction overlaps across calendar month boundaries.<br><br>• Fragile legacy Excel VLOOKUP formulas breaking during file refreshes.                                | • Overprocessing<br><br>• Motion (Searching)<br><br>• Defects | Causes model instability, missing records, and duplicate transaction risks until manual reconciliation is complete. |
| **Data**        | • Inconsistent column schemas between legacy multi-year workbooks.<br><br>• Master Data volatility (`Company Data.xlsx`), where active/resigned employee status changes break lookup tables.                                                                                                     | • Defects                                                     | Triggers M-Code pipeline ingestion errors (`#N/A` breaks) and corrupts financial variance aggregations.             |
| **Technology**  | • Absence of centralized ETL infrastructure (manual spreadsheet copying).<br><br>• Reliance on hardcoded local drive paths (`D:\Projects\...`) without path parameterization.                                                                                                                    | • Overprocessing<br><br>• Non-Utilized Talent                 | Prevents scalability, breaks portability across team workstations, and blocks automated refreshes.                  |
| **People**      | • Skilled FP&A analysts spending >80% of cycle time on manual data manipulation rather than strategic analysis.<br><br>• Lack of standardized file naming conventions across business units.<br><br>• High key-person dependency and operational vulnerability during team leave or transitions. | • Non-Utilized Talent                                         | Model execution relies heavily on individual tribal knowledge rather than an automated, self-healing pipeline.      |
| **Measurement** | • Time-study baselining confirms manual cleansing required ~42 hours per month.<br><br>• Historical reliance on lagging post-hoc manager reviews rather than active daily SPC monitoring ($P_{50} = 1.140\text{ hrs}$, $747,253\text{ DPMO}$).                                                   | • Inventory (Lagging Information)                             | Establishes the quantitative business case baseline and proves ROI achieved through complete pipeline automation.   |
| **Environment** | • Information silos between Accounting (Actuals), HR (Employee Statuses), and FP&A (Budgets).<br><br>• Operational pressure spikes during month-end and quarter-end close windows.                                                                                                               | • Inventory (Lagging Information)                             | Creates cross-departmental friction and delays final delivery of board-level variance reports.                      |
